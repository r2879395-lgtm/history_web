Phase 4: Consolidation of Base Areas

Northern & Central Plains Dominance (Early 1948)

Phase 5: The Three Great Campaigns

The Strategic Blunder (September 1948 - January 1949)

The critical period featuring the Liaoshen, Huaihai, and Pingjin campaigns. These massive engagements saw the KMT lose its elite forces and most critical urban centers, guaranteeing the CCP's ultimate victory.

Phase 6: The Yangtze Crossing

The Collapse of the South (April - May 1949)

Study the PLA's successful crossing of the Yangtze, effectively splitting the Nationalist resistance. Focus on the fall of Nanjing (the KMT capital) and the subsequent capture of major cities like Shanghai.

Phase 7: The Final Outcome

Establishment of the PRC and Retreat (June - December 1949)

The culmination of the war, marked by Mao Zedong's declaration of the People's Republic of China (PRC) in Beijing and the final, permanent retreat of Chiang Kai-shek and the KMT forces to Taiwan.

Understanding the Chinese Civil War requires a clear comparison of the two principal antagonists, the KMT and the CCP, across five critical areas.
